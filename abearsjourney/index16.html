Mr. Peanut waxed poetic: "One of your ancestors, Rumi Bear, once wrote, 'in your light, I learn how to love; in your beauty, how to make poetry - you live inside my heart, so deep inside my heart, where noone sees you, but sometimes I do, and that sight becomes this art.'” As he spoke, an image of the Sufi poet appeared above his paw. #abearsjourney #50

“And who," said Cooksie, "or what," added Peanut, "will be that light inside for you, Bear?" As they spoke, the image of the poet glowed and hovered between their outstretched paws. "For Rumi it was a higher power," Cooksie continued, "But of course that is not the only way," Peanut said thoughtfully. #abearsjourney #51
